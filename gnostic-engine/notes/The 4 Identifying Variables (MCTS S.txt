 The 4 Identifying Variables (MCTS Sequence)
In a supply chain exploit search, the MCTS sequence explores these specific "identifying variables" to determine the exploit's viability:
Dependency Depth (V1): The number of layers deep the target package sits (Transitive risk).
Maintainer Entropy (V2): The frequency and reputation of commits (Social engineering/compromise risk).
Network Surface (V3): Does the package make external calls or require specific permissions?
Version Drift (V4): The gap between the installed version and the most secure/current version.
🌾 Data-PBFP: Foraging Logic
The Data-PBFP (Pre-Buffer Foraging Processor) acts as the pneumatic intake. It doesn't "think"; it filters. It takes raw, noisy data bits and reduces them to localized binary logic (Monads).
Foraging: High-volume ingestion of raw metadata.
Monad Isolation: Splitting data into independent bits so a "bad" bit doesn't infect the overarching synaptic network.
