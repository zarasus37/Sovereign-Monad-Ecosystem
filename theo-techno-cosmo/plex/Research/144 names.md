\## 1. The Matrix: The Parting of the Red Sea



The 72 Names of God are not words found in a lexicon; they are extracted directly from the text of the Torah. Specifically, \*\*Exodus 14:19, 20, and 21\*\*.



In the original Hebrew, each of these three consecutive verses contains exactly \*\*72 letters\*\*. They describe the climax of the Exodus: the Angel of God moving behind Israel, the pillar of cloud blocking Pharaoh's army, and Moses stretching his hand out to divide the sea.



To extract the Names, Kabbalists arrange these three verses as a cryptographic grid using a method called \*boustrophedon\* ("as the ox turns when plowing"):



\* \*\*Row 1 (Verse 19):\*\* Written normally, \*\*Right to Left\*\*.

\* \*\*Row 2 (Verse 20):\*\* Written in reverse, \*\*Left to Right\*\* (representing \*Gevurah\* or severe cosmic judgment).

\* \*\*Row 3 (Verse 21):\*\* Written normally, \*\*Right to Left\*\*.



By reading downward through the columns, you generate \*\*72 unique three-letter combinations (triads)\*\*. These 72 triads are the raw 72 Names of God.

\## 2. The Transmutation into Angels



These three-letter roots represent pure divine currents. To turn these currents into functional, articulate celestial entities (Angels), a divine suffix is mathematically appended to the triad based on the nature of its consonants:



\* \*\*\*El\* (אל)\*\* — Representing mercy, expansiveness, and the sphere of Chesed.

\* \*\*\*Iah\* / \*Yah\* (יה)\*\* — Representing wisdom, illumination, and the sphere of Chokmah.



For example, the very first three-letter root generated from the grid is \*\*Vav-Heh-Vav (והו)\*\*. By adding the suffix, it becomes the Angel \*\*Vehuiah (והויה)\*\*.
