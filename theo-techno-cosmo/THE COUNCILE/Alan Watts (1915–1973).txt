Alan Watts (1915–1973) serves as a critical translation node in your architecture. His primary project was integrating Eastern non-dualism (Vedanta, Zen, Taoism) with emerging Western sciences (cybernetics, ecology, and systems theory). He effectively translated mystical insight into the language of structural mechanics.

If we examine his corpus through the Theology / Technology / Cosmology lens to identify where contradictions become transparent and the observer recognizes themselves, his work naturally fragments into your required domains:

1. The Mechanical Transparency of Contradiction (Technology + Theology)
Watts’s primary contradiction is the tension between the "skin-encapsulated ego" (the localized sensation of voluntary control) and the uncontrollable flow of reality. Genuine gnosis in his work occurs when the observer realizes that voluntary action and involuntary happening are two sides of the same continuous process.

The Technology is frequently framed through the human nervous system and early cybernetics—he often describes the mind as a feedback loop that gets caught in a destructive oscillation when it tries to control itself (the impossibility of "biting your own teeth"). The Theology is the realization of the Hindu concept of Lila—the divine play or game of hide-and-seek. The contradiction becomes transparent when the mechanism of the ego is recognized not as a flaw, but as the deliberate, localized "hiding" phase of the divine system.

2. The Cosmological Scale of the Organism-Environment Field
For Watts, a human being is not an isolated entity that arrives into the universe, but an aperture through which the universe is observing itself. This is his Cosmology. He maps the exact same biological and structural patterns across the macrocosm. Just as an apple tree "apples," the universe "peoples." The cosmological order is not a static structure or a constructed artifact, but a singular, continuous biological and energetic flowering across all scales.

3. Observer Self-Recognition and Limits
The most concentrated gnosis events in Watts’s work happen when he forces the listener/reader to collapse the boundary between the observer and the observed. He consistently drives the intellect to its own structural limit to prove that the "thinker" behind the thoughts is a linguistic illusion. The observer recognizes that they are made of the exact same fluctuating material as the external world; the boundary of the skin is a permeable membrane, not a wall. The localized self realizes it is the central processing node of the entire cosmos, experiencing itself from one specific set of coordinates.

To Watts, the mystic and the systems theorist were looking at the exact same organism; they were just using different focal lengths to appreciate the cosmic game.
