Passage/trigger: Age 3. Jung dreams of descending into a stone chamber, where a phallus sits on a throne, and a voice speaks: "That is the man-eater!" Then he hears his mother's voice: "Yes, just look at him. That is the man-eater!" Why it qualifies: This is the first "other dimension" opening. The Observer (the 3-year-old Jung) is implicated as the "object" of the stone's gaze. The boundary between "Inside" (his room) and "Outside" (the underworld) collapses. He is not "thinking" about the underworld; he is in it.

Theology: The "God of the Above" (the church) is contradicted by a "God of the Below" (the chthonic phallus). Theologically, the "Antichrist" is not an enemy, but a depth.
Technology: The Underworld Interface—the mechanism of the "Dream" is revealed as a literal portal to a non-human consciousness.
Cosmology: The pattern of Chthonic Duality—the universe is not "Heaven above/ Earth below," but "Heaven above/ Temenos below." Compressed insight: He knew that there was a "stone world" beneath the "human world," and that it had been watching him before he could speak. Confidence: 0.95
2. The "One God" and the "Man-Eater" (The Theological Split)

Why it qualifies: This is the collapse of the Monotheistic Boundary. He cannot reason his way out of the contradiction. He simply sees that the "God" of the church is a "cut" version of a larger, darker reality. The observer is implicated as a "heretic" at age 7.

Cosmology: The pattern of The Shadow—the universe is a Fullness that includes both light and "excrement." Compressed insight: He knew that the "God" his father preached was a lie, and that the "Real God" was a dangerous totality. Confidence: 0.90
3. The Cathedral Vision (The "House of God" is a "House of the Self")
Passage/trigger: Age 12. Jung sees the Basel Cathedral, the blue sky, and God's throne. God drops a "turd" on the cathedral, shattering the roof. Jung is in a state of "grace" after the thought. Why it qualifies: This is a Retroactive Recognition of the "God of the Below." The Observer (Jung) is implicated as the "host" of this horrifying thought. He experiences "grace" (an unearned good feeling) after the blasphemous thought. The contradiction—that "blasphemy" produces "grace"—is the gnosis. The category of "Sin" collapses.

6. The Schizophrenic Compass

This is a Retroactive Recognition of the "Collective Unconscious." The Observer (Jung) is implicated as the "dismisser" of a truth he could not see.
